Synopsis

This project involved data analysis on early departures to capacity constrained airports that is having impact on average delay of arrivals. Early departures from domestic airports to these capacity constrained airports being the primary reason for this congestion, was studied and analysed. Synopsis

This project implements prediction of schedule variation and aircraft landing time based on Multi Linear Regression models with minimal attributes using machine learning from historical data of previous flight operations. Air traffic congestion and air delay are two significant issues faced by the capacity constrained airports. Growth of scheduled flights and increased number of passengers per year will lead to an increase in air traffic congestion. If proper monitoring and controlling measures are not taken for schedule variation (delay/early) of arrival, the congestion will get worse in the near future. Flight schedule variation is due to certain factors such as inbound flight delay, weather condition, turn-around delay and unpredictable flight maintenance. This project involved tracking of critical operational factors which influence scheduled variations and modelling of a predictive algorithm to forecast scheduled arrival variations due to the effect of different phases of flight. The variables that influence arrival time variation (early, on time and delay) were found and used to predict the arrival time using machine learning. The flight delay prediction was treated as a regression problem in which the delay was predicted using independent variables.

Synopsis

This project implements an adaptive algorithm for filtering and fusion of multiple heterogeneous sensors, which includes secondary surveillance radars at different geographical location and Automatic Dependent Surveillance-Broadcast (ADS-B), whose measurements and sensor characteristics are different from one another. Decentralized fusion architecture based on 4D-dimensional (3-D plus time) Earth-Centered Earth-Fixed (ECEF) common coordinate system was adapted to process the data received asynchronously from multiple heterogeneous sensors. The implemented algorithm, removes sensor bias, by proper sensor registration process using LMS (Least Mean Square) algorithm and thereby increasing the quality of the track. A decentralized Adaptive filter with Decentralized Kalman Filter Fusion (ADKFF) method based on Mahalanobis distance carries out the fusion task. This project also makes use of the Down-linked Aircraft Parameters (DAP) which can be obtained from mode-S radar and ADS-B for the computation-ally efficient fusion process. The results indicate that the proposed adaptive filtering algorithm with decentralized Kalman filtering can remove sensor registration error, better tracking performance, eliminating ghost and more accurate position information using different types of surveillance sensors.

my Ph.D. Thesis Summary

Performance Improvement of Air Traffic Flow Management and 4D-Trajectory Prediction Using Machine Learning

In this thesis, I studied areas of performance improvement in Air Traffic Flow Management and 4D Trajectory Prediction and developed several data-driven models for (i) Estimated Arrival Time Prediction (ii) Estimated Departure Time Prediction (iii) Optimisation Ground Delay Program and (iv) Aircraft Trajectory Prediction and Strategic Trajectory Conflict Detection. This research analysed actual traffic scenarios in Indian Airspace, various traffic patterns and scheduled flight characteristics before proposing numerous data-driven models to satisfy present and future ATFM planning requirements for optimal capacity, efficiency, predictability and safety.


  • The research addressed the Arrival time prediction problem for scheduled flights. Unlike other systems that gather and use all available features, we efficiently employed feature engineering to determine the effectiveness of derived features in Arrival Time prediction. The capabilities of the Exponential Moving Average in tracking temporal variation in flying time were effectively exploited for arrival time prediction.
  • A machine learning based model for In Block Time prediction and arrival scheduled variation ( Early/On Time /Delay) classification has been proposed.
  • A novel time-based computation method for estimating landing time based on aircraft trajectory characteristics has been developed. A model has been developed based on this for landing time prediction which relies on random variation in the flying time of scheduled flights. This has been implemented using M5P and RF machine learning approaches.
  • A machine learning-based method for predicting the departure time of scheduled aircraft has been developed. The model, which is based on M5P and MLP algorithms, can identify departure delays and forecast departure status (Early/On Time/Delay) depending on the preceding leg departure time.
  • Utilising the improved landing and departure times, a novel algorithm for optimising the Ground Delay Program using Mixed Integer Linear Programming has been developed
  • A Bi-LSTM model has been created for predicting 4-D trajectory and enhancing ETA prediction. The model is capable to predict Short term and Long term 4D trajectories.
  • For future trajectory-based ATFM planning, a model for representing 4D trajectory and its protected airspace on a hexagonal grid has been proposed. Based on this, a novel model for strategic conflict detection based on hexagonal grind indexing has been developed.
